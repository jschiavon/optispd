Parametrization

Let y∈ℝdy\in\mathbb{R}^d, we say that y∼𝒮𝒩d(ξ,Σ‾,δ)y\sim\mathcal{SN}_{d}\left(\xi, \bar\Sigma, \delta\right) if p(y∣ξ,Σ‾,δ)=∫0∞2ϕd+1([y⊤,z]⊤∣μ,Ω)dz\label{eq:first} p(y\mid \xi, \bar\Sigma, \delta) = \int_0^\infty 2\phi_{d+1}\left(\left[y^\top, z\right]^\top\mid \mu, \Omega\right)dz with μ=[ξ⊤,0]⊤\mu=\left[\xi^\top, 0\right]^\top and Ω=(ωΣωωδδ⊤ω1)\Omega=\begin{pmatrix} \omega\Sigma\omega & \omega\delta\\ \delta^\top\omega & 1 \end{pmatrix} and Σ‾=ωΣω\bar\Sigma = \omega\Sigma\omega is the decomposition of the covariance matrix in correlation matrix and the diagonal matrix with variances.

By defining θ=ωδ\theta= \omega\delta and Ψ=Σ‾−θθ⊤\Psi = \bar\Sigma - \theta\theta^\top, we can rewrite the previous density as p(y∣ξ,Σ‾,δ)∝∫0∞2ϕ1(z)ϕd(y∣ξ+θz,Ψ)dz.\label{eq:second} p(y\mid \xi, \bar\Sigma, \delta) \propto \int_0^\infty 2\phi_1(z)\phi_d\!\left(y\mid \xi+\theta z, \Psi\right)dz. Note that |Ω|=|ωΣω−ωδδ⊤ω|=|ω(Σ−δδ⊤)ω|=|Ψ|\left\vert\Omega\right\vert = \left\vert\omega\Sigma\omega - \omega\delta\delta^\top\omega\right\vert = \left\vert\omega\left(\Sigma-\delta\delta^\top\right)\omega\right\vert = \left\vert\Psi\right\vert and Ω−1=(Ψ−1−Ψ−1θ−θ⊤Ψ−11+θ⊤Ψ−1θ)\Omega^{-1} = \begin{pmatrix} \Psi^{-1} & -\Psi^{-1}\theta\\ - \theta^\top\Psi^{-1} & 1 + \theta^\top\Psi^{-1}\theta \end{pmatrix}

Moreover, by rearranging the terms from equation [eq:second] we can write: p(y∣ξ,Σ‾,δ)∝∫0∞2ϕ1(z∣μ‾,σ‾2)ϕd(y∣ξ,Ψ)exp[μ‾22σ‾2]dz=ϕd(y∣ξ,Ψ)exp[μ‾22σ‾2]2∫−μ‾/σ‾∞ϕ1(z)dz=2ϕd(y∣ξ,Ψ)exp[μ‾22σ‾2]Φ1(μ‾σ‾)=2ϕd(y∣ξ,Ψ)exp[12(y−ξ)⊤αα⊤(y−ξ)]Φ1(α⊤(y−ξ))=2ϕd(y∣ξ,Ψ−αα⊤)Φ1(α⊤(y−ξ))\begin{aligned} p(y\mid \xi, \bar\Sigma, \delta) &\propto \int_0^\infty 2\phi_1(z\mid\bar\mu,\bar\sigma^2) \phi_d\!\left(y\mid \xi, \Psi\right) \exp\left[\frac{\bar\mu^2}{2\bar\sigma^2}\right]dz\\ &= \phi_d\!\left(y\mid \xi, \Psi\right) \exp\left[\frac{\bar\mu^2}{2\bar\sigma^2}\right] 2 \int_{-\bar\mu/\bar\sigma}^\infty\phi_1(z)dz\\ &= 2\phi_d\!\left(y\mid \xi, \Psi\right) \exp\left[\frac{\bar\mu^2}{2\bar\sigma^2}\right]\Phi_1\!\left(\frac{\bar\mu}{\bar\sigma}\right)\\ &= 2\phi_d\!\left(y\mid \xi, \Psi\right) \exp\left[\frac{1}{2}(y-\xi)^\top\alpha\alpha^\top(y-\xi)\right]\Phi_1\!\left(\alpha^\top(y-\xi)\right)\\ &= 2\phi_d\!\left(y\mid \xi, \Psi - \alpha\alpha^\top\right) \Phi_1\!\left(\alpha^\top(y-\xi)\right) \end{aligned} where we have used μ‾=(y−ξ)⊤Ψ−1θ1+θ⊤Ψ−1θσ‾2=(1+θ⊤Ψ−1θ)−1\begin{aligned} \bar\mu &= \frac{(y-\xi)^\top\Psi^{-1}\theta}{1 + \theta^\top\Psi^{-1}\theta} & \bar\sigma^2 &= \left(1 + \theta^\top\Psi^{-1}\theta\right)^{-1} \end{aligned} and we defined α=Ψ−1θ1+θ⊤Ψ−1θ\alpha = \frac{\Psi^{-1}\theta}{\sqrt{1 + \theta^\top\Psi^{-1}\theta}}

Constraints

In order for Ψ\Psi (and thus Ω\Omega) to be positive definite, a constrain should be put on δ\delta and Σ‾\bar\Sigma. First of all, recall that the matrix θθ⊤\theta\theta^\top has only one strictly positive eigenvalue, equal to ‖θ‖2\left\Vert\theta\right\Vert_{}^2, while all the others are 0. As it can be proven that Ψ\Psi is SPD if and only if the smallest eigenvalue of Σ‾\bar\Sigma is larger than ‖θ‖2\left\Vert\theta\right\Vert_{}^2, we can require that ‖θ‖2=δ⊤ωωδ≤miniλi(Σ‾)\left\Vert\theta\right\Vert_{}^2 = \delta^\top\omega\omega\delta \leq \min_i\lambda_i(\bar\Sigma)

Data generation mechanism

To generate samples from a skewnormal we exploit equation [eq:first] and we proceed in the following way:

  • We compute μ\mu and Ω\Omega from the parameters ξ\xi, Σ‾\bar\Sigma and δ\delta

  • We generate a sample from a (d+1)(d+1)-variate normal distribution: Z∼𝒩d+1(μ,Ω)Z \sim \mathcal{N}_{d+1}(\mu,\Omega)

  • if Z[d+1]≥0Z[d+1] \geq 0 then y=Z[:d]y = Z[:d], else y=−Z[:d]y = - Z[:d].
